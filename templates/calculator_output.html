Sizing Results

Air-source heat pumps typically lose heating capacity as it gets colder outside because it becomes more difficult to “pump” heat. At the same time, the building heat loss increases as it gets colder outside. For any given outdoor temperature, the heat pump capacity curve must be greater than the building heat loss if it is to be used as the only source of heating for the home. If the heat pump capacity is lower than the heat loss then it will not be able to maintain the indoor thermostat set-point on its own.

The outdoor temperature where the heat loss curve for the home intersects the heat pump capacity curve is the lowest possible temperature where the heat pump can be used exclusively and still meet the home’s heating requirements. This sizing calculator estimates that intersection point. If the back-up heating source is a furnace then, below that intersection, the heat pump must be turned off and the furnace used exclusively. If the back-up heating source is electricity then it is possible for both the heat pump and electric heating coil to be used together with the electric coil “topping up” the temperature provided by the heat pump.
